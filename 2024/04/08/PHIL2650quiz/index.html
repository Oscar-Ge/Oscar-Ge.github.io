Mohist Emphasis on Relational virtues and particularist Ties

The Mohists never deny the values of relational virtues, such as xiao (filial piety) or zhong (loyalty).

“Jian’ai” VS “Ren’ai”

Ren’ai: Xiao is the priorty, in conflicts, Ren would be abandoned.

Jian’ai: especially against the prevailing Confucian love based on kinship(亲属关系).

Mencius critique: Jian’ai not give particularist ties the moral priority they are due in his view.

Confucian familism: priorize the well-being of one’s loved ones over any other moral consideration, including not harming others.

Xunzi and Legalism

Han Feizi, and Li Si are both students of the COnfucian thinker Xunzi.

One of the dilemmas in Confucius: between zhong and xiao

hard to be adopted by rulers

Ritual tradition in Confucianism 礼制

Overview of Xunzi’s reasoning:

  1. Human nature is evil.

  2. Rituals are necessary to civilize human beings.

  3. Rulers are necessary and most important for rituals.

  • Rituals’ roots: Heaven and Earth; forefathers; rulers and teachers

  • former kings established rituals (Later kings are even more important)

  • Rulers are like people’s parents, but more important (给他们礼教).

e.g.:父能生之，不能养之；母能食之，不能教诲之；君者，已能食之矣，又善教诲之者也。

The perfect man, who identifies himself with the universe and follows the nature of things, is absolutely free. The happiness of the finite things is limited, since they haveto depend upon something in order to be happy.

The transformation of life and death is to him but as the succession of day and night, and cannot affect him, to say nothing of the worldly gain and loss, good luck and ill luck.
